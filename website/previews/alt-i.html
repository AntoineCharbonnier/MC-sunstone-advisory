Independent Advisory

The path to the right advisory.

Marine Charbonnier built Sunstone from the inside of both investment banks and private equity firms. Here is what that means for your transaction.

The journey
The journey

Every advisory relationship is shaped by the advisor's vantage point. Most have seen deals from one side only. Marine has seen them from both — and that changes everything.

2006
Sciences Po · Université Paris Dauphine

The Foundation

A dual education in political economics and finance at two of France's most rigorous institutions. The analytical framework that would later make the difference when both sides of the table needed to be understood at once.

Deutsche Bank · Investment Banking

Learning to structure a deal.

As an Associate in Deutsche Bank's Investment Banking division, Marine was in the engine room of transaction execution. Valuations built from first principles. Financial models tested under pressure. Seller processes run from mandate to closing.

What this taught her

"How a deal is structured, financed, and sold — and why most processes are won or lost in the preparation."

2008
2012
The Carlyle Group · European Buyout

Learning to think like an investor.

At Carlyle's European Private Equity fund, the lens shifted entirely. As Senior Associate, Marine evaluated over a hundred acquisition opportunities, negotiated terms, managed portfolio companies, and ultimately prepared and led exit processes — sitting on the other side of every banker pitch she had once given.

What this taught her

"What investors are actually looking for — and the gap between what sellers present and what buyers need to hear."

Sunstone Advisory · Paris
2024

Advising from both sides.

Sunstone Advisory is built on the conviction that the best transaction support comes from having been on both sides of the table. Not just knowing how processes work, but understanding how investors actually think, what they challenge, and what makes them move.

What this enables for you

"A transaction partner who has structured the deal you are selling, and sat in the room where it would be bought."
